The Future is Now

Virtual Reality has evolved from experimental technology to a mainstream medium with 171 million active users worldwide. Major platforms like Meta Quest 3, significant enterprise investments, and breakthrough applications in healthcare, education, and social connection have transformed VR from gaming peripheral to essential digital medium.

In this course, you'll learn to design, not just develop for VR. We emphasize creative experimentation over technical perfection, giving you the skills to craft spatial experiences that leverage VR's unique affordances.

What You'll Learn

During this course, you will:

  1. Master Multiple Creation Platforms: From no-code tools like Horizon Worlds to professional development in Unity
  2. Capture Real Performance: Use our professional OptiTrack motion capture system to bring life to virtual characters
  3. Design for Comfort: Understand the psychology and physiology of VR to create experiences that delight, not nauseate
  4. Think Spatially: Move beyond screen-based thinking to environmental storytelling
  5. Critique Thoughtfully: Develop vocabulary and frameworks for analyzing VR experiences
  6. Integrate AI Ethically: Use AI tools transparently to enhance, not replace, creative work

Course Structure

Weeks 2-5: Platform Exploration

  • Create without coding in Horizon Worlds and VRChat
  • Capture and integrate motion capture performances
  • First critical analysis of VR experiences

Weeks 6-10: Unity Development & Creative Labs

  • Follow the Create with VR course for foundations
  • Weekly creative challenges ("Impossible Physics," "Synesthetic Design," "Emotional Spaces")
  • Build technical skills while maintaining design focus

Weeks 11-15: Final Projects

  • Choose your platform (Unity/VRChat/Horizon)
  • Develop your vision with instructor support
  • Showcase during final exam period (December 1st)

Who Am I?

I'm Dr. John Murray, and I've been exploring the intersection of technology and creativity since before VR's current renaissance. My research investigates how platforms and tools shape creative experiences. I co-authored Flash: Building the Interactive Web and Adventure Games: Playing the Outsider, and co-founded an AR platform startup.

I believe VR represents a fundamental shift in how we create and experience digital media. In this course, we'll explore that shift together - not as master and students, but as co-explorers of a still-emerging medium.

My teaching philosophy: Your creativity matters more than your code quality.
